What changed?

A transparent register of reporting changes, unresolved discrepancies, freshness, and known unknowns.

Goals no longer reported

Three goals went silent in 2025: TR-GAS-G1, FO-G1, and CI-G1 appeared in both earlier annual reports but not the 2025 report.

Inspect these goals

Funding changes

TR-D-G1’s funding classification was corrected after five external primary grant records established a defensible link. The interface marks this as externally verified rather than City-reported funding.

Contradictions register

40% greenhouse-gas threshold

The 2024 report’s prose says the threshold was reached in 2023; its own table shows 2023 at −39.31% and 2024 at −42.13%. This project follows the table for charting and preserves the discrepancy. A corrected City report would resolve it.

Live evidence added

Six live public data sources now cross-check specific claims in real time: GTA's own transit schedule/vehicle data, City Council's Legistar voting record, Census ACS tract-level poverty data, USAspending.gov federal award drawdown status, EPA air-quality readings, and the City's own building-permit system. Known unknowns

  • Fifteen goals have no goal-specific 2025 update.
  • Absence from reporting cannot establish that implementation stopped.
  • The updated official 2022–2024 greenhouse-gas inventory was contracted but not present in this data snapshot.
  • Some funding may exist without a defensible published goal-level link.
