Shared initiatives

See where City departments, OSR, utilities, funders, and community organizations contribute to the same outcome—and where the public record cannot safely divide the credit.

This is an attribution crosswalk, not an assessment. It does not score departments, change goal statuses, divide grant dollars, or treat participation as proof of equal responsibility.

Collaboration summary

How to read attribution

  • Documented: a source explicitly names the participants and relationship.
  • Mixed: some links are explicit and others require cross-referencing.
  • Inferred: records show a shared dependency, but not a formal collaboration.
  • Unresolved: sources conflict or omit participant identities.

Use this with OSR, Shree, and department teams

Reviewers can confirm a participant's role, supply a primary source, identify a missing collaborator, or explain an organizational boundary. Proposed changes continue through the existing evidence review process; they are not published as fact until supported.
